I believe that a service that operates at a greater than 30 minute frequency is unuseable. With Adam Bain's final release of ptmapmelb.com, I decided to have a look at all of the infrequent routes within (approximately) 10km of the CBD, and more importantly, what to do about each one of them.

As part of selecting the routes, I ignored peak-only buses and those that combine over a corridor for a greater frequency.

## Inner Suburbs

**Route 236** -  
Every 40 min, Lots of unique coverage. Terminate at South Melb Market.

**Route 409**  
Every 40min

**Route 609**  
Hourly. Connects a school, rehabilitation centre, community centre, etc. Keep as a special route.

## Western Suburbs

**Route 414**  
40min frequency.

**Route 415**  
40min frequency.

**Route 422**

## Northern Suburbs

**Route 407**  
35min frequency.

**Route 469**  
40min frequency. Straighten.

**Route 476**  
40min frequency. Straighten.

**Route 404**  
40 minute frequency. Short route.

**Route 468**  
40 minute frequency. Short route.

**Route 483**  
Worse than hourly. Runs express between Diggers Rest and Airport West, only stopping at Keilor Golf Course (Which the 421 could be routed to serve). Split the route. 

**Route 509**  
Hourly. Very short route. Reallocate resources to another route or run one bus all day.

## Eastern Suburbs

**Route 284**

## Southern Suburbs

**Route 624**  
Every hour. Re-allocate resources to create a frequent Bourke Rd and Malvern Rd bus route as seen here.

**Route 600**  
Northern part of the route is peak only. Reallocate all 600 resources to a super frequent Sandringham-Southland route along Bay Road.

**Route 922/923**  
Each hourly. Merge into one route (with deviations) and ideally shorten to Elsternwick.

**Route 823**  
Runs worse than hourly. Straighten along Nepean Hwy and run between Moorabin and North Brighton.
